HISTORICAL PLACES & MONUMENTS

  1. S H A N I W A R W A D A

    Shaniwarwada was constructed in 1732 by the Peshwa Baji Rao I as the seat of the Maratha Empire. The fortification played a crucial role in the administration and military strategies of the Maratha rulers. The fort features impressive gates like the Delhi Gate and the Mastani Gate, and though much of it was destroyed by a fire in 1828, the remaining parts still showcase the grandeur of Maratha architecture. Currently, it's a popular tourist attraction and hosts light and sound shows that narrate the history of the Maratha Empire.

  2. AGA KHAN PALACE

    Built by Sultan Mohammed Shah Aga Khan, the palace is significant for its role during the Indian freedom struggle. Mahatma Gandhi, his wife Kasturba Gandhi, and other leaders were imprisoned here from 1942 to 1944. The palace is noted for its Italianate architecture with beautiful gardens and spacious interiors. It features elegant arches and large verandas. It serves as a museum with exhibits related to Gandhi’s life and the freedom movement, and the site also houses the tomb of Kasturba Gandhi.

  3. S I N H A G A D

    Sinhagad, meaning "Lion's Fort," was a key fort in the Maratha Empire, known for its strategic location. It was famously captured by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1670. The fort's architecture includes strong fortifications, bastions, and gateways. It offers a stunning view of the surrounding region, including the Pune plateau. It’s a popular trekking destination and a site of historical re-enactments and celebrations.

  4. R A J G A D

    Rajgad was the first capital of the Maratha Empire, serving as the administrative center before being replaced by Shaniwarwada. It was strategically important for controlling the surrounding regions. The fort features several structures like the Bale Killa (the main fort) and various bastions, all built with a focus on defense and surveillance. Rajgad is a popular site for trekking and historical tourism, with ruins that offer insights into Maratha fortification techniques.

  5. PARVATI HILL & TEMPLE

    Parvati Hill is one of the oldest parts of Pune and is associated with the Peshwa era. The hill and its temples reflect the religious and cultural aspects of the time. The Parvati Temple, dedicated to the goddess Parvati, is a significant temple with intricate carvings and an elevated location that provides a panoramic view of the city. The hill is a popular spot for both religious devotees and tourists, offering scenic views and a serene environment.
